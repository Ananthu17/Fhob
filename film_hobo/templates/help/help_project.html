Projects
Releases, Consents, Permissions
As a Producer or a Project Creator, you need to have all necessary consents, permissions and/or releases signed by the Talent and Crew you’ve attached to your project before you start the production. You cannot use their names, voices, performances or likeness in Your Content without their signed release form!
Scene Projects
Why Scene? Since it only requires one location, it is easier and faster to produce with minimal resources.
Scenes are eligible for Showcase (FilmHobo monthly festival). If a Scene is rated 4 stars or above it will automatically be submitted to the Showcase.
Benefits: (a) Helps actors to find their perfect character to showcase their talent; (b) Lets writers see their work performed and get noticed by producers and film professionals; (c) Helps filmmakers promote their films; (d) Helps filmmakers finesse their craft and to try different roles to advance their career with minimum risk; (e) Helps members build their SAMR quickly to get to the top of the food chain.
Who can create: Indie, Pro and Company.
Who can participate: Indie and Pro.
Pilots and Features
These two are commercial projects, the end goal for any film professional.
Who can create: Pro and Company only.
Who can participate: Indie and Pro.
Creating a new Project
  1. Go to Projects, and then click on “Create a Project”.
  2. Fill out the form; create a cast description and their sides; create the team/crew positions for FilmHobo members to apply for; and then post your project on the projects’ page to be discovered.
Films made prior to FilmHobo
If you made films in the past, you could post those films, or scenes from those films on the FilmHobo portal and get credits/ratings for those films/scenes.
To do that, create a new Project and attach the team which worked on your film originally, and then post the video of that film on the Screening Room page. You will need to invite all the team members if they want to get credit for their work. If their accounts are not active, they will not be able to get the credit and the rating they deserve.
Your Network/Friends are important for your success. When you post a project or get attached to a project, you want people to see and rate it. You will rely on your peers in your network to support you.
