Rating and Credits
What is Rating?
Rating is your currency. It shows your skill level to the whole FilmHobo professional community. The higher your rating is, the more desirable you are.
The rating will be judged by a five stars system, where five stars is the best and one star is the worst. The total rating will be calculated as an average of all ratings combined from each role you have participated in.
Only premium members can rate your work.
To build your rating fast use Scene projects. Usually, it takes years to build your professional resume, but with FilmHobo Scenes production you can do it in no time. Always, choose a scene that is the best to showcase your talent and your abilities.
How do I get Credit?
To get credit as an actor or director you must participate in any project on the FilmHobo platform as an actor or director.
The moment you get attached to a project (for any position) you will get the related credit. For example – if you’ve been attached to a project as an actor and editor, you will get two credits “Actor” and “Editor”. Those credits will be posted on your personal page, as well as the related rating.
You can state your previous jobs or your skills in your bio, but you will only get credit after you’ve been attached to a project on the FilmHobo site.
Crew Credits
Crew will be rated by the filmmakers and co-workers of the projects the crew members have been working on. As a crew member you should ask your co-workers and filmmakers to rate your work.
What is SAMR?
SAMR stands for Status And Minimum Rating, where your Status is your Membership such as Indie, Pro or Comp.
Applying SAMR for your projects
You can limit who can apply for Cast and the Team using SAMR on the project application form.
Example:
  1. SAMR: Pro Rating: 4 Stars+
    2. That means that only members with Pro Membership and with a rating of 4 stars or above can apply for that job.
    Or
  2. SAMR: All Rating: 2 Stars+ That means that all members (Indies and Pros) with a rating of 2 stars or above can apply for the job.
Applying SAMR for contact limitations
SAMR option gives Filmmakers and Companies the ability to control who can contact them through their personal or Company’s pages. Go to settings to set up SAMR contact limitations.
Turning off Rating Visibility
For any reason, if you don’t want to display your rating you can turn it off using settings. Your rating will be “visible” to the FilmHobo system though. So, when you apply for a job or contact a Company, and your SAMR is not meeting their requirements you will not be able to apply or contact them.
